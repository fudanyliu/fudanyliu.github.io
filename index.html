Profiles

I am pursuing the Ph.D. at Fudan University, supervised by Prof. Liang Song. I received my B.S. degree as an honored undergraduate from Nanjing University in 2020. My research interests include causal machine learning and computer vision, focusing on online evolutive learning and video anomaly detection. Currently, I am an algorithm intern in the RF Perception Group of Shanghai AI Lab. I plan to join the Toronto Intelligent Systems Lab at UofT and work with Prof. Igor Gilitschenski as a visiting Ph.D. student.

Generalized Video Anomaly Event Detection: Systematic Taxonomy and Comparison of Deep Models

This review extends the narrow VAED concept from unsupervised video anomaly detection to Generalized Video Anomaly Event Detection, which provides a comprehensive survey that integrates recent works based on different assumptions and learning frameworks into an intuitive taxonomy.

June, 2022 IEEE ICME
Learning Appearance-Motion Normality for Video Anomaly Detection

We propose spatial-temporal memories augmented two-stream auto-encoder framework, which learns the appearance normality and motion normality independently and explores the correlations.
Cite DOI
Yang Liu, Jing Liu, Jieyu Lin, Mengyang Zhao, Liang Song
May, 2022 IEEE TCAS-II
Appearance-Motion United Auto-Encoder Framework for Video Anomaly Detection

We propose an appearance-motion united auto-encoder (AMAE) framework to jointly learn the prototypical spatial and temporal patterns of normal events.
Cite DOI
Yang Liu, Jing Liu, Mengyang Zhao, Shuang Li, Liang Song
April, 2022 IEEE TCAS-II
Collaborative Normality Learning Framework for Weakly Supervised Video Anomaly Detection

We introduce the underlying thought of unsupervised VAD to the weakly supervised VAD and propose a collaborative normality learning framework to obtain more discriminative deep representations.
Cite DOI
Yang Liu, Jing Liu, Xiaoguang Zhu, Donglai Wei, Xiaohong Huang, Liang Song
April, 2022 IEEE ICASSP
Learning Task-Specific Representation for Video Anomaly Detection with Spatial-Temporal Attention

We propose a spatial-temporal attention mechanism to learn inter- and intra-correlations of video clips, and the boosted features are encouraged to be task-specific via the mutual cosine embedding loss.
